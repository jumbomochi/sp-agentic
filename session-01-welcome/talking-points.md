## 2. What is Generative AI — Quick Recap (3 min)

> Skip the basics they already know. Focus on the framing that sets up the rest of the day.

### Key points (not a full intro — they know this)
- Generative AI creates new content (text, code, images) rather than just classifying or predicting
- LLMs (Large Language Models) are the engine behind tools like Gemini
- The quality of what you get out depends on how you prompt — which they already know

### The shift to focus on today
- "You already know how to prompt. Today we're going beyond prompting to *building with AI*."
- "The question isn't 'Can AI write my email?' — it's 'Can AI run my workflow?'"

## 3. The Google Gemini Ecosystem (7 min)

### The five pillars — where each fits

Present these as a landscape, not a feature list:

| Product | What it is | Best for |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Gemini app** | Standalone AI assistant | Brainstorming, deep research, image/video generation, canvas |
| **Google Workspace with Gemini** | AI embedded in Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, Meet, Drive, Chat | Enhancing daily productivity inside the apps you already use |
| **NotebookLM** | AI research assistant grounded in your documents | Synthesising insights from multiple sources without hallucination |
| **Google Vids** | AI-powered video creation | Creating training videos, presentations, and announcements |
| **Vertex AI** | Google Cloud's AI/ML platform | Building LLM-powered applications, testing prompts at scale, using the Gemini API |

> "This morning we'll work with the first three. This afternoon, we move into Vertex AI."

### How they connect
- Gemini (the model) powers all of these — same brain, different interfaces
- Workspace with Gemini = Gemini embedded in your daily tools
- Vertex AI = Gemini as a development platform
- The day follows this progression: use → customise → automate → develop

## 4. From Prompts to Applications — The AI Maturity Spectrum (8 min)

### Draw or display this spectrum:

```
PROMPTING  →  CUSTOMISING  →  CODE AUTOMATION  →  NO-CODE PIPELINES  →  DEVELOPING APPS
   │               │                 │                    │                    │
 Gemini app     Gems            Apps Script         Workspace             Vertex AI
 Side panels    Custom           Triggers            Studio Flows          Gemini API
               instructions     Scheduled            Ask Gemini +          Python/code
                                workflows            Actions
```

### Walk through each level with an SP example:

**Level 1 — Prompting:** "Ask Gemini to draft an email to students about the upcoming CA test."

**Level 2 — Customising:** "Create a Gem called 'Feedback Writer' that always gives constructive, rubric-aligned feedback on student submissions. You set up the instructions once — then use it every time."

**Level 3 — Code Automation:** "Use Apps Script (generated by Gemini) to automatically email personalised grade notifications to every student in your spreadsheet. It runs on a trigger — you don't even need to press a button."

**Level 4 — No-Code Pipelines:** "Build a Workspace Studio Flow that automatically classifies incoming student emails using Gemini and pings you in Chat for urgent ones — a no-code visual pipeline where AI is a first-class step."

**Level 5 — Developing Apps:** "Use the Gemini API through Vertex AI to build a custom application — like a grading assistant or a data analysis tool that your department can deploy."

> "Today we'll touch every level on this spectrum. That's the journey."

## 5. Responsible AI in Education (5 min)

### Google's AI principles (brief)
- Bold innovation, collaborative progress, responsible development
- Data privacy: Google Workspace with Gemini does not use your data to train models
- Transparency: AI-generated content should be reviewed before use

### Hallucination awareness
- LLMs can present incorrect information confidently
- Mitigation strategies we'll practice today:
  - **Grounding** (NotebookLM, Workspace Flows, Vertex AI) — constraining responses to known documents
  - **Verification** — always review AI outputs, especially for factual claims
  - **Guardrails** — prompts and instructions that say "if you don't know, say so"

### Academic integrity
- "AI is a tool for *you* as educators and administrators — not a shortcut for students to bypass learning"
- Consider: How do you use AI to create better teaching materials while maintaining assessment integrity?
- SP context: Be aware of your institution's AI use policy for both staff and students

### Data privacy for student data
- Do NOT paste student personal data (NRIC, grades, contact details) into the public Gemini app
- Google Workspace with Gemini has enterprise data protections — but always check your institution's data governance policy
- In today's exercises, we use sample/mock data for demonstrations
