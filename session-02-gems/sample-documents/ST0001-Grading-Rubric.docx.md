# ST0001 Statistics I — Assignment Grading Rubric

**For use by lecturers and tutors when assessing student work**
**School of Mathematical Sciences and Analytics, Singapore Polytechnic**

---

## Overview

This rubric applies to tutorial assignments, CA test open-ended questions, and any written statistical analysis tasks in ST0001 Statistics I. Each criterion is scored independently on a 4-point scale.

---

## Criterion 1: Statistical Understanding (30%)

| Score | Descriptor | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| 4 — Excellent | Demonstrates thorough understanding of statistical concepts | Correctly identifies the appropriate method. Explains why the method is suitable for the data and research question. Connects concepts to underlying theory (e.g., explains assumptions of a t-test). |
| 3 — Good | Demonstrates solid understanding with minor gaps | Selects the correct method and applies it appropriately. May not fully articulate why the method is suitable or may miss a minor assumption. |
| 2 — Developing | Demonstrates partial understanding | Selects a method that is partially appropriate. Shows some understanding of the concept but confuses key details (e.g., uses a one-tailed test when two-tailed is required). |
| 1 — Beginning | Demonstrates limited understanding | Selects an inappropriate method or applies the concept incorrectly. Significant misconceptions are evident. |
| 0 — Not attempted | No evidence of understanding | Question not attempted or response is irrelevant. |

---

## Criterion 2: Computation and Accuracy (30%)

| Score | Descriptor | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| 4 — Excellent | All calculations are correct and clearly presented | Correct numerical answers. Working is shown step-by-step. Intermediate values are clearly labelled. Final answer is boxed or highlighted. Units are included where applicable. |
| 3 — Good | Calculations are mostly correct with minor errors | One minor arithmetic or rounding error that does not affect the conclusion. Working is shown. |
| 2 — Developing | Calculations contain errors that affect the result | Correct approach but multiple computational errors. OR correct setup but incorrect execution. Partial working shown. |
| 1 — Beginning | Calculations are largely incorrect | Significant errors in computation. Little or no working shown. Answer appears guessed. |
| 0 — Not attempted | No calculations provided | Question not attempted. |

---

## Criterion 3: Interpretation and Communication (25%)

| Score | Descriptor | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| 4 — Excellent | Clear, complete interpretation in context | Interprets results in the context of the original question (e.g., "There is sufficient evidence at the 5% significance level to conclude that the mean weight differs from 500g"). Uses correct statistical language. Distinguishes between statistical significance and practical significance. |
| 3 — Good | Clear interpretation with minor omissions | Provides a correct interpretation but may lack full context or use slightly imprecise language (e.g., "We reject the null hypothesis" without stating what that means in context). |
| 2 — Developing | Interpretation is present but incomplete or generic | Provides a generic statement (e.g., "The result is significant") without connecting it to the problem context. May confuse accept/reject language. |
| 1 — Beginning | Interpretation is absent or incorrect | No interpretation of results. OR misinterprets the conclusion (e.g., states "we accept the null hypothesis" instead of "we fail to reject"). |
| 0 — Not attempted | No interpretation provided | Question not attempted. |

---

## Criterion 4: Presentation and Organisation (15%)

| Score | Descriptor | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| 4 — Excellent | Exceptionally well-organised and professional | Logical flow from problem statement → method → calculation → interpretation. Clear headings or structure. Graphs/charts are properly labelled (title, axes, units). Tables are formatted with headers. Easy to follow. |
| 3 — Good | Well-organised with minor formatting issues | Generally clear structure. Most labels present. One or two formatting issues (e.g., missing axis title on a chart). |
| 2 — Developing | Some structure but difficult to follow | Work is partially organised but lacks clear flow. Missing labels on charts or tables. Reader must work to understand the logic. |
| 1 — Beginning | Poorly organised or difficult to read | No clear structure. Calculations scattered without labels. Charts or tables missing key elements. |
| 0 — Not attempted | No work presented | Question not attempted. |

---

## Feedback Tone Guidelines

When providing feedback to students, the lecturer/tutor should:

- **Lead with what the student did well** — even struggling students will have something positive to reinforce
- **Be specific about errors** — instead of "wrong method," say "you used a z-test here, but since the population standard deviation is unknown and n < 30, a t-test would be more appropriate"
- **Suggest next steps** — point students to specific lecture notes, tutorial questions, or resources that address their gaps
- **Use encouraging language** — "You're on the right track with the setup. Focus on..." rather than "You failed to..."
- **Distinguish between conceptual and computational errors** — a student who understands the method but makes an arithmetic mistake needs different support from one who chose the wrong method entirely

## Grade Conversion

| Total Score (out of 16) | Grade | Descriptor |
|--------------------------|-------|------------|
| 14–16 | A | Excellent — exceeds expectations |
| 11–13 | B | Good — meets expectations with minor gaps |
| 8–10 | C | Developing — meets minimum standard |
| 5–7 | D | Below expectations — significant improvement needed |
| 0–4 | F | Not meeting standard — requires academic support |
