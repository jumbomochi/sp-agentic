Dataset: SP Student Wellness Survey 2025

This dataset was collected by the School of Mathematical Sciences and Analytics as part of a cross-school student wellness research initiative conducted in October 2025.

The survey was administered via Google Forms to all Year 1 and Year 2 students enrolled in diploma programmes under SMSA. Participation was voluntary and anonymous. 847 valid responses were received out of 1,203 students contacted (70.4% response rate).

The dataset contains the following fields:

response_id - auto-generated unique ID for each response, format RES-0001 to RES-0847
timestamp - date and time of form submission, format YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS
year_of_study - either "Year 1" or "Year 2"
diploma_programme - one of: Data Science, Applied Mathematics, Business Analytics, Financial Technology
gender - M, F, or Prefer not to say
age - integer, ranges from 17 to 28
accommodation_type - "HDB", "Condo", "Landed", "Hostel", or "Other"
daily_commute_minutes - integer, self-reported one-way commute time to campus in minutes
sleep_hours_weekday - float, average hours of sleep on weekdays
sleep_hours_weekend - float, average hours of sleep on weekends
exercise_frequency - "Never", "1-2 times/week", "3-4 times/week", "5+ times/week"
stress_level - Likert scale 1-10 where 1 = no stress and 10 = extremely stressed
academic_satisfaction - Likert scale 1-5 where 1 = very dissatisfied and 5 = very satisfied
social_belonging - Likert scale 1-5 where 1 = strongly disagree ("I feel I belong at SP") and 5 = strongly agree
part_time_work - "Yes" or "No"
work_hours_per_week - integer, 0 if not working. ranges from 0 to 35.
financial_stress - "Not at all", "Slightly", "Moderately", "Very", "Extremely"
mental_health_support_aware - "Yes" or "No" (whether they know about SP counselling services)
would_use_mental_health_support - "Yes", "No", "Maybe"
gpa_range - self-reported, categories: "Below 2.0", "2.0-2.49", "2.5-2.99", "3.0-3.49", "3.5-4.0"
preferred_study_method - "Alone", "Study group", "Online resources", "Tutoring/consultation"
screen_time_hours - float, average daily non-academic screen time in hours

Notes:
- 23 responses had missing values in sleep_hours_weekday and were imputed with the median value (6.5 hours)
- The dataset has been anonymised. No personally identifiable information is included.
- The stress_level and academic_satisfaction fields showed moderate negative correlation (r = -0.42) in preliminary analysis
- Commute times ranged from 5 minutes to 95 minutes, with a mean of 38 minutes and standard deviation of 18 minutes
- The data is stored as a CSV file: sp_wellness_survey_2025.csv (file size: 312 KB)
