CONTEXT FOR GEMINI IN DOCS EXERCISE
=====================================

Use the information below to prompt Gemini in Google Docs to generate a tutorial worksheet.

MODULE: ST0001 Statistics I
TOPIC: Confidence Intervals (Week 6)
AUDIENCE: Year 1 diploma students at Singapore Polytechnic

WHAT STUDENTS HAVE ALREADY LEARNED:
- Descriptive statistics (mean, median, mode, standard deviation)
- Probability rules and probability distributions (normal, binomial)
- Sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem
- Hypothesis testing (z-test, t-test, one-tailed and two-tailed)

WHAT THIS WORKSHEET SHOULD COVER:
- What a confidence interval is and how to interpret it
- Constructing confidence intervals for population means (known and unknown σ)
- Constructing confidence intervals for population proportions
- Choosing the correct formula based on the scenario
- The relationship between confidence level and interval width
- The effect of sample size on interval width

WORKSHEET REQUIREMENTS:
- Start with a brief recap of key formulas (CI for mean with known σ, unknown σ, and proportion)
- Include 5-6 practice problems with increasing difficulty
- Problems should use realistic Singapore contexts (e.g., HDB flat sizes, MRT wait times, hawker centre prices, student commute times)
- Include at least one problem that requires students to interpret a CI result in words
- Include at least one problem where students determine the required sample size for a desired margin of error
- End with a "challenge question" that combines confidence intervals with hypothesis testing

TONE: Clear and encouraging. Suitable for students who may be encountering these concepts for the first time. Avoid overly technical language where simpler phrasing works.

FORMAT: Numbered questions with space for working. Include a formula reference box at the top.

---

SUGGESTED EXERCISE PROMPT:
"Generate a tutorial worksheet for Year 1 statistics students on the topic of confidence intervals. Include 6 practice problems using realistic Singapore contexts, a formula reference box, and a challenge question that links to hypothesis testing. Keep the language clear and encouraging."
