## Question

A manufacturer claims that the average weight of cereal boxes produced by their factory is 500g. A quality inspector suspects that the boxes are underfilled. A random sample of 25 boxes has a mean weight of 495.2g and a standard deviation of 8.6g. Test the inspector's claim at the 5% significance level.

---

## My Answer

**Step 1: State the hypotheses**

H₀: μ = 500
H₁: μ < 500

This is a one tail test because the inspector thinks the boxes are underfilled (less than 500g).

**Step 2: Identify the test**

We use a z-test because we are testing the mean.

Test statistic: z = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n)

z = (495.2 - 500) / (8.6 / √25)
z = -4.8 / (8.6 / 5)
z = -4.8 / 1.72
z = -2.79

**Step 3: Find the critical value**

For α = 0.05, one-tailed, the critical value is z = -1.645

**Step 4: Make the decision**

Since -2.79 < -1.645, we are in the rejection region.

Therefore we reject H₀.

**Step 5: Conclusion**

The boxes are underfilled.
